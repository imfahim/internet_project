Market Capitalization is calculated by multiplying the Price by the Circulating Supply. It provides a measure of the relative size of a cryptocurrency in the market.

Circulating Supply: The number of coins in the market.

Total Supply: The total existing coins (minus burned coins).

Max Supply: The maximum coins that will ever exist.

Circulating Supply is used because it represents the coins actively in the market, excluding locked or reserved coins that can't impact the price.
